Mark Zuckerberg

May 14, 1984 (age 38)

Biography

Mark Zuckerberg is a well-known figure in the tech industry, having co-founded Facebook, one of the most popular social media platforms in the world. Born in 1984 in New York, Zuckerberg showed an early interest in computers and programming, which led him to create several software programs before eventually launching Facebook in 2004 while still a student at Harvard University.

As Facebook grew in popularity, Zuckerberg became a billionaire and a household name. He has been credited with revolutionizing the way people connect and communicate online, and Facebook has become an integral part of many people's daily lives. However, Zuckerberg has also faced criticism for his handling of user data and privacy concerns, as well as accusations of allowing the spread of false information and hate speech on the platform.

Despite the controversies surrounding Facebook, Zuckerberg has continued to lead the company and has become a prominent figure in the technology industry. He has also become known for his philanthropic efforts, including the creation of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, which aims to advance science, education, and equal opportunity.

Overall, Mark Zuckerberg is a complex and influential figure in the tech world, with a legacy that will continue to shape the way we interact with each other online for years to come.
